MONKEY

"Monkey" is a general term that often refers to mammals within the infraorder Simiiformes, also known as simians. Traditionally, all members of the group excluding apes were considered monkeys, creating a paraphyletic grouping. However, in a broader sense based on cladistics, apes (Hominoidea) are also included, making "monkeys" and "simians" synonymous in scope.

Monkey species exhibit diverse lifestyles, with many being arboreal (tree-dwelling), while others, like baboons, primarily inhabit the ground. Most monkeys are active during the day (diurnal). They are generally recognized for their intelligence, particularly Old World monkeys.

Monkeys, along with apes, are characterized by having only two pectoral nipples, a pendulous penis, and an absence of sensory whiskers, features that differentiate them from other primates.
